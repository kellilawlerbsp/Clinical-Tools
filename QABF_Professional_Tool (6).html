About This Assessment

The QABF is a standardized tool to identify the function(s) maintaining behaviour of concern. It consists of 25 items rated on a 0-3 scale across five behavioral functions: Attention, Escape, Non-Social, Physical, and Tangible.

Before You Begin:

  • Identify one specific behavior to assess (e.g., "hand biting", "screaming", "property destruction")
  • Complete separate assessments for different behaviors
  • Base ratings on actual observations, not assumptions
  • Consider recent patterns (last 1-2 weeks)
  • Allow approximately 15-20 minutes to complete

Interpretation Guide

  • High (≥66.7%): Strong indication this function maintains the behaviour
  • Moderate (33.3-66.6%): Possible contributing function
  • Low (<33.3%): Unlikely to be maintaining the behaviour

Radar Chart Interpretation: The "shape" of the radar chart shows whether behavior is singly maintained (one dominant peak) or multiply maintained (multiple elevated scores). This helps guide multi-component intervention strategies.

Reference: Paclawskyj, T.R., Matson, J.L., Rush, K.S., Smalls, Y., & Vollmer, T.R. (2000). Questions About Behavioral Function (QABF): A behavior checklist for functional assessment of aberrant behavior. Research in Developmental Disabilities, 21, 223-229.
